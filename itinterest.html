My Interest in Information Technology

Why do I love IT?

Information Technology has been the cornerstone of my life in virtually every way.

Like a lot of young people of my generation, I was glued to our family computer growing up. I fell in love with how I could learn almost anything via academic computer software and how I could communicate with my friends and loved ones via gaming together, creating and distributing animations and other fun projects with one another and be together over social media.

I've always been fascinated by how people use Information Technology to benefit human-kind by bringing out new and exciting inventions almost every day and I can't wait and see how the introduction of new and upcoming technologies will shape our future as a species.

How I've used IT in my previous careers

Whilst I haven’t had a career based solely on IT, I had been fortunate enough to be with the New Zealand Police for around four and a half years, where IT was vital in everything we did.

As an example, I was required to securely make queries into suspects for investigation, update intelligence databases in real time for all other officers to access and transmit secure information over encrypted digital radio and telecommunication channels on a daily basis.

Criminal activity is beginning to rise with the abundance of Information Technology, from fraudulent matters as simple as obtaining cash by deception for selling a false product over social media to significant money laundering and wire money transfer scams.

My dream is to move into a career based solely on stopping this.

Why did I choose RMIT University?

As I wanted to return home to Australia to be with my family and friends again, I decided to find a career based on online forensic fraud prevention and security.

Whilst I’m currently working full-time, I decided to dedicate my remaining time on following my passion with IT and justice and mix them into one.

RMIT University has provided me with the opportunity to start working towards my dream career, as it allows me to work and study at the same time. I am entirely commmited on completing my degree in order to gain the knowledge required to transfer my skills into the IT workplace.

I'm hoping to get into and complete a Bachelor of Computer Science, and upon completion of this degree, I will be looking at transferring into a career based in IT.

My goal is to then complete a Master of Information Technology, Computer Science or Cyber Security whilst working in my new IT role to ensure I end up achieving my goal of becoming a fraud analyst or cyber security expert.
